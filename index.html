Question & Answer

Do I need a computer science degree to become a developer?

No, most developers today are self-taught or bootcamp graduates. What matters is your skills and portfolio, not your degree. This roadmap gives you everything you need to learn without a formal education.

How long does it take to become a frontend developer?

With consistent daily practice, most people are job-ready in 6 to 12 months. It depends on how much time you dedicate each day and how quickly you build real projects alongside your learning.

Should I learn frontend or backend first?

We recommend starting with frontend. HTML, CSS, and JavaScript give you visible results immediately which keeps you motivated. Once you're comfortable, you can move to backend or go fullstack.

What programming language should I start with?

JavaScript is the best first language for web development. It works on both frontend and backend, has a huge community, and is the most in-demand language for web developers worldwide.

Do I need math skills to become a developer?

For most web development roles, you do not need advanced math. Basic logic and problem-solving skills are far more important. Math becomes relevant only in specific fields like game development, AI, or data science.

How many hours a day should I study?

Even 1 to 2 focused hours a day will get you far if you stay consistent. Consistency matters more than long study sessions. Burning out after a week of 10-hour days is worse than steady daily progress.

When should I start building projects?

As early as possible. Do not wait until you feel ready because that moment never comes. Start building small projects after your first two weeks of learning. Projects teach you more than any tutorial ever will.

Is the Professional plan worth it?

If you are serious about getting hired quickly, yes. The 1-on-1 mentorship, mock interviews, and CV review directly address the biggest obstacles developers face when job hunting for the first time.

Can I switch between frontend and backend tracks?

Absolutely. Many of our learners explore both tracks and become fullstack developers. Your progress is saved across both roadmaps so you can switch at any time without losing where you left off.

What if I get stuck on a topic?

Every plan includes access to our community where you can ask questions and get help. Advanced and Professional plan members also get priority support, and Professional members can book a direct session with a mentor.
